There are numerous resources to search for scholarships. However, looking through local scholarships should be your first priority. Remember to recycle essays!

Nov 1 - Nov 30
Early Action / Decision Applications Due

Some private colleges offer early action and decision, this usually means an earlier application deadline but you will receive your decision by Janurary. Early Action is often non-binding but it may be the case you can only apply to one early action school. Early Decision is binding and can only be broken due to financial reasons.

Nov 30
UC and CSU Applications Due

Applications to the all UC and CSUs are due on Nov 30th. No exceptions. SAT Scores do not have to be officially sent and received by Nov 30th. Once your application has been received, all colleges will send you a link to create a student portal. It is very important you do so since this portal is where the colleges contact you with important information about your application status.

  • Apply to UCs
  • Apply to CSUs
Nov 30 - Jan 30
Educational Opportunity Program App Due

Educational Opportunity Program (EOP) is a college resource for low-icome, first generation college students. While it varies from schools, EOP can provide students their own academic counselor, registration priority, a small scholarship, and many helpful workshops.

For UCs, the application for EOP was part of the UC application itself. For CSUs, the application is linked here. Make sure you find out the deadlines for each school as they differ. Letters of recommendation and short answers are required for the application.

Dec - May
Community College Applications Open

Community College applications vary when they open and often times they do not inform students or the schools. Constantly check for updates on each school's website for up to date information. Most community college application uses a CCC account, so you can create that early.

Regular Decision for Private Schools Due

Most private schools have regular decision deadlines either Jan 1st or Jan 15th. Letters of recommendation should be also be submitted by these dates.

  • Go to Common App
Mid Jan - Late Jan
Mid Year Reports and SAT Scores Due

All privates, University of California, and California State Universities require a mid year report which is essentially your transcripts with Senior Fall Term semester grades. Your counselor is the one who submits these, so please check your college's student portal to see if it is missing.

All SAT and ACT scores should have been submitted and sent to all colleges. Check college's student portal to see if the college has received yours.

Feb - May
CSU EPT and ELM Testing

Going into college, there will always be placement tests. CSUs require you to take these placement test sometime between February and May. However, there are ways you are exempted from these placement test through SAT and ACT scores. Refer to the chart below. If you need to take the test, you will need to pay $18 per test and you can only take it once. Take it seriously!

March 2
California Deadline for FAFSA/Dream Act

The FAFSA is the Free Application For Student Aid. You should never pay for this application. The FAFSA gives all colleges essentially a picture on how much they and the govenmern can give you in aid and how much they expect you to pay out of pocket. If you want the most financial aid and be qualified for the Cal Grant, you must complete the FAFSA by March 2nd. All students who want to continue education after high school must complete this. To file, you and your parents must provide SSN and Tax Forms (if applicable).

If you do not have a SSN, you can still apply for financial aid with the Dream Act. There are some requirements such as qualifying as AB540. These requirements are listed here.
